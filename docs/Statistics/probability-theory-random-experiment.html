Random Experimentâ€‹

The study of probability originates from experiments with uncertain outcomes, referred to as random experiments.

  1. A random experiment is an experiment whose outcome cannot be predicted in advance.
  2. A random experiment can be repeated under identical conditions.
  3. The probability of a random experiment can be given by the number of favourable outcomes / total number of outcomes.

Sample Spaceâ€‹

The sample space is the set of all possible outcomes of a random experiment.

It is denoted by SSS, and it is the universe of all possible outcomes.

Sample Pointâ€‹

A sample point is a specific outcome of a random experiment. It is denoted by xxx, and it is a member of the sample space SSS.

Discrete Sample Spaceâ€‹

A sample space is called a discrete sample space if all its sample points can be put into a one-to-one correspondence with the set of positive integers(N\mathbb{N}N).
